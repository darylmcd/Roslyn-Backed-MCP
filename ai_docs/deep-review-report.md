## Complexity Hotspots

Methods with cyclomatic complexity > 20:

| Method | CC | LOC | Nesting | Params | File |
|--------|---:|----:|--------:|-------:|------|
| `InterfaceExtractionService.PreviewExtractInterfaceAsync` | 35 | 199 | 6 | 7 | InterfaceExtractionService.cs:25 |
| `MutationAnalysisService.ClassifyTypeUsage` | 31 | 75 | 1 | 1 | MutationAnalysisService.cs:265 |
| `UnusedCodeAnalyzer.FindUnusedSymbolsAsync` | 29 | 97 | 4 | 5 | UnusedCodeAnalyzer.cs:23 |
| `DependencyAnalysisService.GetDiRegistrationsAsync` | 25 | 92 | 4 | 3 | DependencyAnalysisService.cs:138 |

### Flow Analysis Detail

- **`PreviewExtractInterfaceAsync`** (CC=35): 57 declared local variables, 2 captured in lambdas (`typeName`, `nameSet`), single exit point at line 222. This method badly needs decomposition — the 57 locals and 199 LOC indicate multiple responsibilities packed into one method.
- **`ClassifyTypeUsage`** (CC=31): 12 declared locals, 17 return paths, 0 captured variables. This is a pure pattern-matching classifier — structurally simple despite high CC. Could benefit from a switch expression but is functionally clean with no side effects.

### Recommendations

1. **Extract methods** from `PreviewExtractInterfaceAsync` to reduce the 57 local variables. Candidate sub-methods: type declaration finding, member filtering, interface source generation, solution diff computation.
2. **Consider switch expression** for `ClassifyTypeUsage` — would reduce line count while preserving the classification logic.
3. `FindUnusedSymbolsAsync` and `GetDiRegistrationsAsync` could each be decomposed into smaller scanning/filtering steps.
